Cats

What are Cats?

Cats are domestic animals that are used as pets by humans. They’re very popular pets for humans. They can have very different appearances and behaviors. They can be found on all continents on Earth other than Antarctica.

What is their lifespan?

The lifespan of cats has increased across the world. Currently, cats live for about 15 years. Some cats are reported to have survive for about 30 years.

Appearance

Cats are small animals when compared to humans. They have fur for protection against cold weather. They also have claws, which can scratch objects. These claws make cats very difficult opponents to fight. They also have a strong, flexible body, quick reflexes, sharp teeth, and “retractable” claws, meaning they can be drawn back into the toe in a special sheath.

Characteristics

Its night vision is very good because of its unique retina, which makes its eyes glow at night with a strong light. This allows it to see properly in the darkness. They are also very good listeners. Their ears have 30 muscles, where as humans only have 6.

Number of Cats in the world

As of 2021, there were an estimated 220 million owned cats and 480 million stray cats in the world. As of 2017, the domestic cat was the second most popular pet in the United States, with 95.6 million cats owned and around 42 million households own at least one cat. In the United Kingdom, 26% of adults have a cat, with an estimated population of 10.9 million pet cats as of 2020.

Foods that Cats eat

Cats are meat eaters, plain and simple. They have to have protein from meat for a strong heart, good vision, and a healthy reproductive system. Cooked beef, chicken, turkey, and small amounts of lean deli meats are a great way to give them that. They are also well known to drink milk. They also prey on rats & fish, and the former could help you get rid of rats in your house.
